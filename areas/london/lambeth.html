Funeral singers and choirs in Lambeth

From the historic grounds of Lambeth Palace to the vibrant communities of Brixton, Streatham, and West Norwood, the London Borough of Lambeth is a place of rich contrasts. We provide professional singers and choirs for funerals, weddings, and memorial services at churches, crematoria, and ceremony venues across the borough.

Funeral music in Lambeth

Lambeth’s churches and ceremony venues span centuries of history, and our singers bring the same care and professionalism to every one of them. St John’s Waterloo, with its elegant interior and fine organ, is a popular choice for funeral and memorial services close to the South Bank, while Lambeth Palace Chapel — the private chapel of the Archbishop of Canterbury — occasionally hosts services of exceptional significance. Further south, St Luke’s West Norwood offers a welcoming parish setting for families in the Norwood and Tulse Hill area. Our experienced ensembles understand how to tailor their performance to the unique acoustic and atmosphere of each venue. For help choosing music, please see our funeral music guide or contact us to discuss your needs.

West Norwood Cemetery, one of the “Magnificent Seven” Victorian cemeteries of London, is a remarkably atmospheric setting for graveside services and committal rites. We also provide singers for funeral services in Streatham and across the southern reaches of the borough, working alongside funeral directors to ensure seamless coordination on the day. Whether the occasion calls for a single hymn or a full choral programme, we will help you find the right musicians and repertoire. Learn more about what we offer on our services page.

Wedding choirs in Lambeth

Lambeth’s churches provide beautiful and distinctive settings for weddings, and a professional choir can make the ceremony truly memorable. St John’s Waterloo is a particularly elegant choice, with its spacious nave and excellent acoustics, while smaller parish churches in Brixton, Clapham, and Streatham offer a more intimate feel. Our singers work with couples to build a programme that reflects their tastes — whether that means traditional hymns, classical anthems, or something more contemporary. For ideas and inspiration, see our guide to choosing wedding hymns.

Ensembles for every occasion

Not every occasion calls for the same size of ensemble, and part of our job is helping you find the right balance for your venue, your budget, and the feeling you want to create. We offer everything from a single soloist — whose voice can hold a room in complete stillness — through to duets, quartets, octets, and a full twelve-voice chorus with instrumental accompaniment. Organists, harpists, and string players are also available. Visit our pricing page for a clear guide to ensemble sizes and costs.
